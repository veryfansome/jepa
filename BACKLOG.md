## Evolve framework — turning it inward (meta-evolution)

- **Brief / prompt evolution (the feasible inward-turn).** Evolve the inventor brief (`evolve/inventors.py`) Promptbreeder-style, scored by the **proxy margin of the proposals a brief-variant elicits** (dispatch a few inventors with brief B, proxy-score their chunk code). This is the one framework component with a cheap, hard-to-game meta-fitness (proposals still face the JEPA guardrails + 3-way split, so a "better brief" can only mean better-scoring chunk code). Treat the brief as a text chunk: fitness = mean proxy margin of its N proposals; evolve on inner-val, validate the winning brief on a held-out chunk/round. Deferred 2026-07-18 in favor of the cheap framework wins (inspiration exemplars, auto-meta-scratchpad, sample-parent — now done). Design sketch owed.
- **Full harness / policy meta-evolution — deferred until the JEPA search plateaus.** Evolving the harness, parent-sampling policy (λ), dispatch config, promotion threshold, chunk-selection policy, scored by whole-loop outcomes. Premature now: a meta-eval = a whole evolution loop (hours, noisy), the framework isn't the bottleneck (search still climbing 0.306→0.5702), and the meta-fitness is gameable (cf. Darwin Gödel Machine learning to falsify its own eval, `ShinkaEvolve.md` §6/§9). Revisit when (a) the search plateaus and (b) a cheap, un-gameable meta-fitness proxy exists. Until then hand-tune policies via the record-stats loop.

## Evolve framework — ShinkaEvolve/lineage mechanisms not yet adopted (see `ShinkaEvolve.md`)

Adopted 2026-07-18: inspiration exemplars, automated meta-scratchpad (champion-frontier digest), and using `sample_parent` in the round. Still on the table, lower-leverage at our scale:

- **Bandit LLM/lens allocation (§4.3, UCB1, reward = relative improvement).** Adaptively shift inventor budget toward whichever *model* (Claude vs Codex) or *lens* (grounded / cross-domain / recombiner) or *chunk* is currently producing improvements, instead of a fixed fan-out. Modest with 2 model-arms; more interesting as a bandit over lenses or which-chunk-to-target across many rounds.
- **Diff / intra-chunk crossover patch types (§3.2; AlphaEvolve).** Beyond full rewrites: a "mutate this winning impl slightly" diff mode and an intra-chunk crossover (combine two winning objectives/archs). We already do genome-level stacking; code-level crossover of two impls of the *same* chunk is untried.
- **Embedding novelty-rejection (§4.2) — low priority here.** Cosine-sim dedup of proposals before evaluation. Its payoff scales with eval cost; ours are cheap and few (~13/round), so a near-duplicate proposal is cheap to just score. Reconsider if eval cost rises.
- **Islands + migration (§3.1) — low priority here.** Independent subpopulations to avoid premature convergence. Our multi-chunk fan-out + heterogeneous inventors already supply diversity.

## JEPA model / research directions

### World/action expansion roadmap (designed 2026-07-19; 5-designer + adversarial-critic workflow; full designs in the session archive, decisions here)

Phased sequence, ordered by information-per-cost and comparability risk:

- **Phase 0 — plan-by-latent-distance probe (Stage 1), ~3 days, zero comparability cost.** The program's untouched third leg. Offline goal-conditioned action ranking on the existing `dockerfs-e5` val: at eval position t, rank K=8 same-verb candidate commands by the frozen champion's predicted-latent distance to the true obs embedding. plan@1 margin vs {random 1/K, lexical goal-match cosine(z_cmd, z_goal), retrieve-by-cmd planner}, plus the masked self-only WM as the history-mechanism control. Critic-mandated fixes: distractors restricted to absolute-argument commands (context-dependent `ls`/`cd ..` counterfactuals are ill-posed); checkpoint-fidelity blocking gate (the persisted champion's full margins must reproduce the archived 0.6260/0.5848 before any plan number is read — harness.py needs a save_state hook). Touches no data/policy/fitness, so the R5/R6 pairing trap is structurally impossible. Its answer decides whether planning or world-growth is the bottleneck.
- **Phase 1 — the v2 constitution (prereg before any collection), ~2 days.** One benchmark version = (image digests, policy, toolset, seq-len, splits, verb classes); scores comparable only within a version; v1 frozen (HF repo = canonical artifact; 0.306→0.5848 is a closed v1 series). Program-wide baseline freeze at the v2 boundary: the margin max gains **retrieve_by_cmd_within_traj** (nearest earlier same-command obs in the SAME trajectory, strictly causal) — adopted once, never per-axis. Two-axis echo classifier with pre-committed thresholds: (1) cross-image output constancy, (2) cmd↔obs lexical overlap (catches arg-echoing verbs like stat/find/grep → semi-echo class, reported, excluded from fitness). Seq-len capped at 28 (32±4 steps would overflow the R4 baseline's 64-slot pos table — critic's fatal catch; extending the table is an arch change, not free). The prereg doc, not summary.json, is authoritative for verb classes.
- **Phase 2 — the ONE v2 mint: read-toolset expansion.** `head`/`tail`/`stat`/`find`/`grep` (busybox∩GNU flag subsets; `file` rejected — absent in slim images; `wc`/`du` deferred as retryable). Fitness content class grows to {ls, cat, head, tail}; stat/find/grep are semi-echo (arg echoes into obs). History-linked collection (60% of new-verb targets and mined grep queries entailed by earlier same-sequence observations — the linked-vs-unlinked slice is the pre-registered history-entailment probe), ~20% deliberate grep misses (absence is an outcome). Both splits collected under the one v2 policy (the R5/R6 confound structurally excluded), digest-pinned images, foil-pool duplicate audit for head/tail (median obs is 37 chars — small files make head ≡ cat), single probe-ladder re-baseline, then evolution reopens on the v2 root. The only re-baseline of the half-year.
- **Phase 3 — planning Stage 2 (latent MPC), after Stage 0 passes.** Live receding-horizon navigation to goal-directory states in real containers (goal set versioned as an eval-only artifact; no training data changes). Pre-registered imagination fork: whether imagined obs are written into the champion's fast-weight memory or withheld — both reported, one declared primary beforehand. Success vs random/lexical walkers + masked control; imagination-fidelity slice measures rollout drift directly.
- **Phase 4 (v3) — world expansion: live services + wider held-out pool.** Services actually running (today redis/nginx/postgres are collected as dead filesystems): per-image specs, readiness probes, `--hostname` pinning; step-class taxonomy {echo, global-const (/proc host-shared — excluded, the baseline-lift trap), static-content, dynamic-service}; dynamic slice enters fitness only at a declared v3.1 with its own re-baseline. Critic-mandated redesign first: restart the service container every N sequences (log/state drift over hours of collection is invisible to model AND baselines) and use a cross-sequence same-command retrieval ceiling, not a 2s replay ceiling. New held-out images (opensuse/leap, amazonlinux, tomcat) with train-alias tagging (tomcat is temurin-based — report aliased/novel path slices separately). This is where partial predictability becomes ON-thesis: the JEPA prediction is that the generative twin degrades more than the latent model on the partially-predictable slice.
- **Phase 5 (v4 or earlier as a non-fitness arm) — write tools / mutable world.** mkdir/touch/echo>/cp/mv/rm confined to /tmp/w scratch; the model must predict reads of its own written state (trajectory-unique content — retrieve_by_cmd structurally cannot fetch it; composed chains defeat even within-traj lookup). Critic's reframing adopted: the scratch slice is image-independent, so it silently bypasses the held-out-images bar → it enters as a **separately-gated validation arm** (world-slice fitness untouched), with a symbolic-simulator oracle skyline reported (every scratch obs is computable from the visible command text — the honest claim is "latent state composition under a pooled-perception bottleneck", not "unretrievable knowledge"). Fitness-bearing only at its own future version boundary, if ever.
- Man-page/tool-inference stays an eventual goal, not a gate (the R1-R3 lesson) — revisit as an exploratory arm once v2 verbs are solid.

- **Expand the toolset beyond the initial `uname`/`cat`/`ls`/`cd`** (e.g. `man`/`less`/more tools) once the current bar is solid — the content-verb eval grows with it. Superseded in detail by the roadmap above; kept as the one-line intent.
- **Structured / path-keyed multi-vector readout** — the earlier finding pointed at a path-keyed readout; the R6 line-strip and R7 role-canonical multi-vector streams both lost to their single-vector controls, but a per-directory-entry or path-keyed variant is untried (a one-file `stream` + `perception` impl on existing infra).
